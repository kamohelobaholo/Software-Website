LION

Lions are large carnivorous mammals belonging to the genus Panthera and are commonly referred to as the "king of the jungle." They are the only cats that live in social groups called prides, which consist of related females, their offspring, and a small number of adult males. Male lions are easily recognised by their impressive manes, which vary in colour and size and serve as a display of health and strength. Historically, lions were found throughout Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia and Europe, but today they are mainly restricted to parts of sub-Saharan Africa and a small population in India known as the Asiatic lion.

Lions have strong muscular bodies with powerful jaws and sharp claws, making them formidable predators. They typically hunt large herbivores like zebras, wildebeest, and buffalo, working together to stalk and ambush prey. Female lions do most of the hunting, while males protect the prideâ€™s territory from intruders and rival males. Lions are known for their loud roars, which can carry up to 8 kilometres and help them communicate with other pride members. They spend a significant portion of the day resting and are most active during the cooler hours of dawn and dusk.

Lions prefer open savannahs, grasslands, and scrublands where prey is plentiful and water sources are accessible.
