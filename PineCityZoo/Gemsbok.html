GEMSBOK

Gemsbok are large antelopes native to the arid regions and open plains of Southern Africa. They are famous for their long, straight, ringed horns that both males and females possess. Gemsbok have striking markings with a light grey body, black-and-white facial patterns, and contrasting dark stripes along their flanks and legs. They are well adapted to hot, dry climates and can survive long periods without direct water by obtaining moisture from the plants they eat.

These antelopes live in herds ranging from small groups to larger gatherings depending on food availability. Gemsbok are grazers and browsers, feeding on grasses, roots, and wild melons. Their horns are used for self-defense against predators like lions and hyenas. Agile and alert, gemsbok can defend themselves fiercely when threatened.
