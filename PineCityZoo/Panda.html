PANDA

Giant pandas are large, bear-like mammals native to the mountainous regions of central China. They are easily recognised by their distinctive black and white fur pattern and their playful, gentle behaviour. Pandas primarily feed on bamboo, which makes up over 99% of their diet, although they may occasionally eat small mammals or carrion. Due to their specialised diet, pandas spend a large part of their day eating and resting. Once widespread throughout southern and eastern China, pandas are now classified as vulnerable, with conservation efforts helping to slowly increase their numbers in the wild.

Pandas have powerful jaws and strong molars to help them crush tough bamboo stalks. They are excellent climbers and often escape predators by ascending trees. Pandas are mostly solitary animals, marking their territory with scent glands and vocal calls to communicate. They give birth to tiny, helpless cubs that stay close to their mothers for up to two years. The giant panda is an international symbol of wildlife conservation and is beloved around the world for its unique appearance and gentle nature. Breeding programs and habitat protection have become crucial for their survival.

Pandas inhabit temperate forests with dense bamboo understories and access to fresh water.
