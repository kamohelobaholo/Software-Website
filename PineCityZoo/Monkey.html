MONKEY

Monkeys are highly intelligent primates found in many parts of the world, from the tropical rainforests of Central and South America to the forests and savannahs of Africa and Asia. They belong to two main groups: New World monkeys, which live in the Americas, and Old World monkeys, found in Africa and Asia. Monkeys vary greatly in size, colour, and behaviour, but they are known for their agility, expressive faces, and social nature. Many species live in groups called troops and communicate through vocal sounds, facial expressions, and gestures.

Most monkeys have long tails, which some species use for balance or even grasping branches in the case of prehensile-tailed monkeys. They spend much of their time in trees, where they search for fruits, leaves, flowers, insects, and small animals. Monkeys are playful and curious, often seen swinging from branches, grooming one another, or using simple tools to gather food. Because of habitat loss and hunting, some monkey species are now endangered, making conservation efforts vital to their survival. Zoos help protect monkeys by educating visitors and supporting breeding programs.

Monkeys thrive in forests, woodlands, and jungle canopies with plenty of trees and food sources.
