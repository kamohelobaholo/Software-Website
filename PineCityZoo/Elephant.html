ELEPHANT

Elephants are the largest land mammals alive today, belonging to the family Elephantidae and the order Proboscidea. Two main species are recognised: the African elephant (Loxodonta africana) and the Asian elephant (Elephas maximus), with African bush and forest elephants sometimes listed as separate species. These gentle giants are found across sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, and Southeast Asia. They have played important roles in cultures, ecosystems, and conservation efforts worldwide.

Elephants are known for their long trunks, large ears, and massive tusks which they use for feeding, digging, and social interactions. Their trunks have thousands of muscles, making them incredibly versatile tools for grasping food and drinking water. Elephants live in tight family herds led by matriarchs and display complex social behaviours and remarkable memory. They are herbivorous and spend much of their day feeding on grasses, leaves, and fruits. Sadly, they face threats from habitat loss and poaching for ivory, making their protection critical.

Elephants live in savannahs, forests, marshes, and grasslands, always staying near water.
