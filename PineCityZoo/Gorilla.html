GORILLA

Gorillas are the largest living primates and are members of the great ape family. They live in dense forests and mountain regions of central Sub-Saharan Africa. Gorillas are highly social animals that live in groups called troops, led by a dominant silverback male who protects and guides the group. They have broad chests, massive shoulders, and strong arms for climbing and foraging. Gorillas are primarily herbivorous, feeding on leaves, stems, fruit, and occasionally insects.

Despite their powerful build, gorillas are gentle and intelligent creatures that communicate through vocalisations, facial expressions, and body language. Mothers are highly nurturing and care for their young for several years. Gorillas spend much of their day foraging and resting in family groups. Sadly, gorillas face threats such as habitat loss, poaching, and disease, making conservation vital to their survival. Zoos play an important role in raising awareness and protecting these remarkable animals.

Gorillas prefer tropical and subtropical forests and mountainous regions with thick cover.
